How to Make a Mehndi Song Medley for Your Big Night

The mehndi is the most playful, colourful night of a desi wedding — and the entrance medley sets the whole tone. Here's how to build a mehndi medley that starts sweet, builds into a dance-floor moment, and has everyone up and clapping.

This guide focuses on song choice and structure for a mehndi. If you want the full step-by-step on the tool itself, start with how to make a wedding song medley — then come back for the mehndi-specific flavour.

The ideal mehndi medley structure

Think of it as a short journey in three acts:

  1. Open romantic (0–30s). A soft, recognisable song to walk in to — something everyone can sing along with.
  2. Lift the energy (30–90s). Move into upbeat, clap-along tracks. This is where the dhol and Punjabi numbers come in.
  3. Peak and drop (final 20–30s). End on your biggest, loudest, most-hyped song so the entrance finishes on a high.

Mehndi song ideas to search

A few crowd-tested starting points you can search and add to your timeline:

  • Romantic openers: Tum Hi Ho, Kesariya, Sajni, Dil Diyan Gallan.
  • Playful mid-section: Pasoori, Nachde Ne Saare, Gallan Goodiyaan, Morni Banke.
  • Dance-floor peaks: Brown Munde, Excuses, Lover, Baraat-style dhol numbers.

These are just prompts — the best medley is the one full of your songs. Search any track or artist and build from there.

Add a signature dhol hook

The trick that makes a mehndi medley feel live: use relay mode to drop a short dhol pattern between sections. Add a beat of silence before the drop and you get that heart-stopping pause right before the beat kicks back in — the moment the whole room reacts.

Keep every clip short and punchy

For a mehndi entrance, tighter is better. Trim each song to its most recognisable 20–30 seconds. You want people thinking "oh I love this song!" every few seconds — not waiting through a verse.
